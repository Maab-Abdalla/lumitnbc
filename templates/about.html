About

Clarity for the TNBC journey

LumiTNBC is a research-driven tool that classifies Triple-Negative Breast Cancer into its molecular subtypes and explains each result in language patients and clinicians can both follow.

What LumiTNBC does

Triple-Negative Breast Cancer is not a single disease. It separates into distinct molecular subtypes — Basal-like 1 (BL1), Basal-like 2 (BL2), Luminal Androgen Receptor (LAR), and Mesenchymal (M) — that behave differently and respond to different therapies. Knowing the subtype is a meaningful part of understanding treatment options.

LumiTNBC takes gene expression data, clinical and pathology information, or a combination of both, and predicts the most likely subtype — then shows the genomic and clinical factors that shaped that prediction.

How it works

The classifier is a gradient-boosted model (XGBoost) trained on combined public datasets. Predictions are paired with SHAP, an explainability method that attributes the result to specific input features — so a classification is never a black box. Patients see a plain-language summary; clinicians can inspect the underlying feature contributions.

Patients can also request a written review of their result from a healthcare provider, keeping a human in the loop.

An important note

LumiTNBC is a research and educational tool. It does not provide a medical diagnosis and is not a substitute for professional clinical judgment. Always discuss results and treatment decisions with your oncology care team.
